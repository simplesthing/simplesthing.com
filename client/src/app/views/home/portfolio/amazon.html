Amazon Widgets

Discover New Music

I contracted for a year with Amazon Creative Services making interactive widgets for the retail site. At the start there were four UX designers and myself and we cranked out jQuery, PERL Mason widgets at a lightening fast pace. Two of the designers wrote the HTML, CSS and some JavaScript while I focused on making them dynamic and connected to Amazon back end services. This was the first time a non development team had made interactive widgets for the site.

The widget above was my favorite, it was called "Discover New Music" and was supposed to help a shopper discover new music like the music they last purchased or browsed. The reason this widget was so much fun was because it combined all the hacks and discoveries our team had made together in our first six months of making interactive widgets. We created an AJAX proxy page as way to expose otherwise internal API's by converting PERL to JSON and sending it back in response, along with appropriate headers, for our widget to consume.

Hero Slider US Hero Slider Japan

Another interesting thing that we did with the widgets were to internationalize them for other countries to use on their domains. I learned about the challenges that language can create. It's not just the act of translation that is an issue, it's the effect that another language has on designs, such as German text needing more width and Asian text needing a little more height to compensate for the random catalog text that will get pulled into the design.
